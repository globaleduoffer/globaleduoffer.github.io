Common Question

Degree Verification vs Degree Apostille

Many applicants confuse academic verification with apostille or legalization. They serve different purposes.

Degree Verification
  • Confirms the authenticity of an academic qualification.
  • Often used for immigration, WES, ECA, employment and university admissions.
  • May involve MOE verification, university confirmation or credential evaluation agencies.
Degree Apostille / Legalization
  • Confirms the authenticity of the document itself for overseas use.
  • Often required by foreign governments, employers or licensing bodies.
  • May involve notarization, apostille or consular legalization.
Degrees obtained in China

For bachelor, master, PhD and other degrees obtained at Chinese universities, we will determine – based on your destination country – whether you need one or a combination of the following:

  • Official degree verification reports issued by the Ministry of Education or designated centers.
  • Credential evaluation reports from overseas agencies recognized by immigration authorities.
  • Official Chinese/English enrolment or graduation letters, course descriptions and other documents issued by your university.

Suitable for: applicants who completed their studies in China and plan to immigrate, work or pursue further education in another country.

Degrees obtained overseas

If you obtained your degree in Europe, North America or other regions and now need to use it in China or a third country, you may need to consider:

  • Completing degree verification for returning graduates, for job applications or civil service exams in China.
  • Obtaining a third-party credential evaluation report for immigration or employment in another country.
  • Arranging official transcripts and letters from your university, combined with notarization / legalization when required.

Suitable for: returnees who studied abroad and plan to work in China, or those who intend to move on to a third country for further study or employment.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Does degree verification always have to be done in the country where I studied?
A: Not always. Some types of verification can be completed online or through agencies in other countries. We design a convenient solution based on where you are now and where you need to use your degree.
Q: Can I reuse my verification report multiple times?
A: In many cases, a verification report can be reused within a certain validity period. However, different institutions have different rules on validity, so it is important to check in advance.
Q: I am still studying. Is it useful to plan ahead now?
A: Yes. The earlier you understand the requirements of your target country or institution, the easier it is to avoid issues with course selection, language scores and internship arrangements later on.

Additional Degree Verification FAQs

Can Chinese degrees be used for Canada immigration?

Yes. Many applicants obtain WES or other ECA reports to demonstrate Canadian educational equivalency.

Can I use my Chinese degree in the USA?

Often yes, but universities, employers and licensing bodies may require a credential evaluation.

What is the difference between WES and MOE verification?

MOE verification confirms educational records in China, while WES evaluates equivalency for overseas use.

Can verified degrees also be apostilled?

Yes. Depending on destination requirements, verified academic documents can also proceed to apostille or legalization.

Additional insight: when degree verification becomes essential

For many immigration programs, overseas job applications, and postgraduate admissions, verified academic credentials are a mandatory requirement. Authorities and institutions need to confirm not only that your Chinese degree is genuine, but also how it compares to local education standards. Incomplete or informally prepared documentation can lead to additional questions, delays, or even refusal of your application.

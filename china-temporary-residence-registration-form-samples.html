Residence Registration Forms

China Temporary Residence Registration Form Samples

City-focused notes for temporary residence registration forms in China and how these records may support China PCC applications.

Overview

A China Temporary Residence Registration Form records where a foreign visitor stayed in China. It is often used as supporting residence evidence for China police certificate applications and other document procedures.

The form name, stamp, layout and fields may vary by city, hotel registration route or police station.

What is a China Temporary Residence Registration Form?

A Temporary Residence Registration Form records a foreign visitor's accommodation address, stay period and identity information in China. It is often used as supporting evidence for China PCC, visa, residence and document verification cases.

Who Needs This?

Former China residents applying for PCC

Residence registration can help prove where and when the applicant stayed in China.

Visa and residence applicants

Some visa, residence permit or immigration processes ask for proof of accommodation history.

Students and employees

People who lived in dormitories, employer housing, rented apartments or hotels may need to verify stay records.

Case reviewers

Law firms, agencies or HR teams can use the sample to understand what information appears on the form.

The sample below shows a redacted Temporary Residence Registration Form issued in Guangzhou. Other cities may use different layouts or online records.

How to Use It

  1. Check the name, passport or certificate number, address in China, check-in date, visa details and police station stamp.
  2. Match the residence dates with the period required by your China PCC or visa application.
  3. If the form is missing, collect alternative evidence such as passport entry stamps, old permits, employer or school records.
  4. For overseas submissions, confirm whether a copy, translation or notarized copy is required.

Common Mistakes

Only keeping the latest form

Some applications require residence history for a full stay period, not just the most recent registration.

Unclear address or dates

Blurred scans, incomplete addresses or missing dates can make the form less useful as evidence.

Relying on a hotel receipt

A hotel booking or receipt is not always the same as official residence registration evidence.

Not matching passport history

Old passport numbers or changed names should be explained when they differ from current documents.

FAQ

Is residence registration always required for China PCC?

Not always, but it is often useful for confirming city, stay period and local records.

Can I use an old registration form?

Possibly. The key is whether it supports the relevant stay period and matches your identity documents.

What if I lost my registration form?

You may still have options using other residence, study, work or passport evidence depending on the city.

What is a China temporary residence registration form?

It is a registration record showing where a foreign visitor or resident stayed in China. It is often issued or registered through a local police station, hotel or approved accommodation system.

Why does residence registration matter for China PCC applications?

Residence registration can help confirm where and when a foreign applicant lived in China, which may affect the city route for a police clearance certificate.

Can I apply for a China PCC if I lost my residence registration form?

In many cases, yes. The local route depends on whether the police station, employer, school, hotel or landlord can help confirm the residence record.

Are residence registration forms the same in every city?

No. Format, language, fields and issuing procedures can vary by city and by the accommodation registration method.

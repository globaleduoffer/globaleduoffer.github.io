Timeline and fee

Processing time for New Zealand immigration cases

In most standard cases, the China PCC can be completed in around 7-10 business days after the required documents are ready. Multi-city history, special police-office checks, translation, notarization, apostille or legalization can add time.

New Zealand Immigration decision guide

New Zealand immigration document decisions before you order a China PCC

New Zealand immigration and residence cases often require careful timing because police certificates are normally used as part of a character assessment. The route should be matched to your visa stage and document request.

Timing
Most standard China-side cases are completed in about 7-10 business days after documents are ready. Extra time may be needed for translation, authentication or international courier delivery.
Validity
Immigration New Zealand or the specific visa instruction decides whether the certificate is recent enough. If your application timeline is still uncertain, confirm timing before ordering.
Document version
Plan whether your file needs a digital copy, original paper certificate, translation, notarization, apostille or consular legalization support.
Country-specific risk
Common New Zealand risks include character-check timing, multi-city China stays, previous passports and confusion over which document version to submit.

Residence and skilled pathways

FAQ

New Zealand China PCC questions

Check My Case

Can I apply after leaving China?

Yes. Most New Zealand immigration applicants can handle the China-side PCC process remotely through authorized representation, depending on the city and residence records.

How long does it take?

Most standard cases can be completed in around 7-10 business days after required documents are ready. Additional authentication or multi-city checks may take longer.

Are fingerprints required?

Fingerprint requirements depend on the Chinese city, local police office and application route. We confirm this before you prepare forms.

Do I need notarization or apostille?

It depends on the certificate format and the New Zealand immigration request. We review whether translation, notarization, apostille or other authentication should be prepared.

What if I lost my registration form?

In many cases the PCC can still be issued. We can contact the local police station or relevant local office to verify or request reissued records where possible.

Can the original be shipped overseas?

Yes. After issuance and any supporting steps, the original documents can be sent by international courier.

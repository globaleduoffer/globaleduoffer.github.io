WES ECA Fee Canada 2026: Documents, Timeline & Application Guide

This guide is for applicants planning a WES Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) for Canada immigration, Express Entry or PR, as well as applicants who need a WES credential evaluation for study, employment or professional licensing. It explains how to check current WES fees, choose the right evaluation type, prepare required documents, arrange official transcript delivery and avoid common delays.

Quick answer: WES fees are confirmed inside your WES account before payment and may change based on the evaluation purpose, delivery method, extra recipients and taxes. For most applicants, the bigger delay risk is not the fee itself, but whether official transcripts and supporting documents are sent in the exact format WES requires.

Step 1: Confirm Whether You Need a WES ECA for Canada or Another Evaluation Type

When is a WES evaluation required?

The following situations often require or recommend a WES report:

  • Applying for Canada immigration, Express Entry or PR where an Educational Credential Assessment may be required;
  • Applying to universities or colleges in the US or Canada, especially for graduate programs;
  • Applying for skilled immigration or professional qualification assessments;
  • Employers or licensing bodies need to verify the equivalency of foreign degrees.

Main WES evaluation types

Different programs require different report types. The most common are:

  • Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) – commonly used for Canada immigration and IRCC-related purposes.
  • Course-by-Course Evaluation – lists each course and credit; often used for admissions.
  • Document-by-Document Evaluation – provides an overall degree equivalency; often used for immigration or employment.

Before placing an order, you should carefully check the requirements of your target schools or institutions, or send them to us so that we can help you choose the most suitable evaluation type.

Step 2: Create a WES Account and Enter Your Education History

Create your account and select the country

Go to the WES website, choose the appropriate program (US or Canada) and create a personal account. Use a long-term, frequently used email address so that you will not miss important notifications.

When selecting the purpose of the evaluation, you can choose options such as “Education”, “Immigration” or “Licensing” according to your situation. Different purposes correspond to different packages and fees.

Add your education history

Follow the prompts to enter your education details, including:

  • Country and city of study;
  • Name of the institution (using the dropdown list when possible);
  • Degree obtained, major, and dates of attendance;
  • Whether you have graduated and obtained the official diploma.

Step 3: Prepare Personal and Academic Documents

Typical documents required

The exact requirements vary by country and degree level, but usually include:

  • Passport bio page or other identity document;
  • Degree certificate and/or diploma;
  • Transcripts (in many cases, WES only accepts transcripts sent directly by your institution);
  • Name change documents if applicable.

Language and translation requirements

WES generally requires documents in English. If your original documents are in another language, you must provide proper English translations. We can help you with certified translations and formatting that follow WES standards.

Step 4: Arrange for Your Institution to Send Official Transcripts

Why must the school send them?

To ensure authenticity, WES normally requires that official transcripts be sealed and sent directly by the institution, education authority or archive office. Transcripts mailed by the applicant are often not treated as official documents.

Delivery methods and tips

  • The envelope must be properly sealed and stamped, and show your WES reference number;
  • Common methods include EMS, DHL, UPS or special channels designated by authorities;
  • Keep your tracking number and regularly check the “Documents received” status in your WES account.

If you are overseas or find it difficult to communicate with your institution, you may authorize us to help coordinate transcript delivery with the school or archive office.

Step 5: Check WES Fees, Submit Your Application Online and Pay

After confirming all information and understanding the document requirements, you can submit the order and review the fee shown in your WES account before payment. The final amount may depend on the evaluation purpose, delivery option, additional recipients, taxes and any current WES policy updates. WES supports multiple payment methods; your bank may charge an additional international transaction fee.

If you are searching for the WES ECA fee for Canada in 2026, use WES Canada as the final source of truth. Old screenshots, forum posts and third-party summaries may not reflect your exact order total.

Once the order is confirmed, WES will generate a detailed “Required Documents” list and your WES reference number. This number must be included with all documents sent to WES.

Step 6: Wait for the Evaluation and Monitor the Status

Processing time and report format

After WES has received all required documents, the evaluation usually takes about 7–20 business days. When it is complete, you can download the electronic report in your account, and WES may also send paper copies to your chosen recipients.

Common reasons for delays

  • Slow delivery or loss of mail from the institution;
  • Inconsistent information between documents, requiring clarification or re-issuance;
  • Documents not meeting the definition of “official documents” and needing to be resent.

If your account shows “On Hold” or “Need Additional Information”, check the explanation carefully and respond as soon as possible. If you have difficulty doing this on your own, our consultants can help review WES messages and suggest solutions.

FAQ: WES ECA Fees, Documents and Processing Time

1. What is the WES ECA fee for Canada in 2026?

WES fees can vary by evaluation purpose, delivery option, extra recipients, taxes and later WES updates. The safest way to confirm the current official total is to create or log in to your WES Canada account and review the amount shown before payment.

2. How long does a WES ECA take after documents are received?

After WES receives all required official documents, many evaluations take about 7-20 business days. Delivery delays, school verification, incorrect transcript format or missing information can extend the timeline.

3. What documents are usually required for a WES evaluation?

Typical requirements include identity information, degree or diploma documents, and official transcripts sent by your institution or approved authority. Exact requirements depend on your country of study, degree level and selected WES program.

4. Can I apply for WES while I am overseas?

Yes. Many applicants start a WES evaluation while living outside the country where they studied. The main challenge is coordinating official transcript delivery and making sure the WES reference number is included correctly.

5. Is it enough to evaluate only my highest degree?

Some programs only require the highest degree, while others prefer a complete education history. You should always follow the official instructions of your target schools or institutions. If you are unsure, you can send us the requirements and we will help interpret them.

6. Can I use transcripts that are already sealed in an envelope?

It depends on who sealed them and how. In some cases WES can accept “official sealed documents” forwarded by the applicant, but if the envelope has been opened or the stamp is not valid, new documents may be required.

7. Can WES send the report to multiple recipients?

Yes. You can add multiple recipients in your WES account, with additional fees depending on the number. You may also purchase additional reports later if you apply to more institutions.

8. What if WES refuses or questions my documents?

First, read the WES explanation carefully to identify whether the problem is with translation, format or the institution’s status. If you need assistance, you can share the message and copies of your documents with us and we will advise on feasible next steps.

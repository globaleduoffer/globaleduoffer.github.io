Testimonials from Clients Around the World

We work long-term with clients who have lived, studied or worked in China, helping them handle China-related police certificates, notarisation and legalisation, degree verification and study-in-China matters. The testimonials below are based on real cooperation experiences; only personal privacy details have been removed or anonymised.

AG
Aisha Garcia
Mexico · Study-in-China Support

I was awarded a provincial government scholarship to study environmental engineering in Wuhan, but the scholarship portal gave almost no guidance on what documents were needed before I could receive my visa letter. Your team contacted the university international office on my behalf, confirmed the exact pre-enrollment checklist, and helped me get my academic transcripts and personal documents translated and notarized in the right format. I arrived with everything in order and my scholarship disbursement started from the first month without any delay.

Daniel Chen
New Zealand · China Police Clearance (PCC)

I had worked in Guangzhou for three years and needed a police clearance certificate from that period for a security clearance process back in Italy. Because I had already left China and my residence permit had expired, I assumed the request would be rejected outright. Your team explained that a valid application was still possible with a notarized power of attorney and confirmed what the Guangzhou public security bureau required for overseas applicants. They handled all communication in Chinese and the certificate reached me in Milan within the timeframe they had estimated.

Your team explained the exact documents the local entry-exit bureau required — including the enrollment certificate, the accommodation registration form, and the physical examination report — and gave me a precise timeline for when to begin the process after arrival. I completed the permit application on my first visit to the bureau without needing to return for any corrections.

Everything was submitted correctly the first time and the evaluation came back within the standard processing window.

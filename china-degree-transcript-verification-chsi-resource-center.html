China Degree & Transcript Verification (CHSI) Resource Center

CHSI/CSSD transcript verification report samples, degree online verification report examples and practical notes for WES, immigration, employment and overseas academic use.

Overview

This resource center explains common CHSI and CSSD verification report samples for China degree and transcript verification, including Chinese and English report formats.

CHSI and CSSD verification reports are often requested for overseas study, immigration, employment, WES, ECA and credential evaluation processes.

What is CHSI / CSSD Verification?

CHSI or CSSD verification is an official China higher education verification route used to confirm degree, diploma and transcript information. It is commonly requested by credential evaluators, universities, employers and immigration-related authorities.

Who Needs This?

Chinese university graduates

Graduates who need to verify a China degree certificate, diploma or transcript for overseas use.

WES and ECA applicants

Applicants preparing verified academic documents for WES, ECA or other credential evaluation agencies.

Universities and employers

Institutions checking whether a CHSI or CSSD report is a valid support document for a Chinese credential.

Apostille and legalization users

People who need academic documents notarized, apostilled or authenticated after verification.

How to Use It

  1. Identify whether the receiving authority needs a degree verification report, transcript verification report, original transcript, translation or all of them.
  2. Check the report language, report number, validity period, QR code and online verification information.
  3. For credential evaluation, confirm whether the report must be sent directly or uploaded by the applicant.
  4. If notarization, apostille or legalization is required, keep the verified academic document route consistent with the destination instructions.

Common Mistakes

Submitting only the degree certificate

Many evaluators require a formal CHSI or CSSD verification report, not just a scanned diploma.

Wrong report language

Some agencies require English reports, while others can accept Chinese reports with certified translation.

Expired or unverifiable report

Online verification reports may have validity periods or verification codes that must remain active.

Mixing transcript and degree requirements

Degree verification and transcript verification are different documents and may both be required.

FAQ

What is the difference between CHSI and CSSD?

CHSI is widely used in English to describe China higher education verification, while CSSD is the current English name used for student services and development center reports.

Do I need both degree and transcript verification?

Many credential evaluation or study applications require both, but the exact requirement depends on the institution or agency.

Can verified reports be used for apostille or legalization?

They may support the process, but some destinations require notarized academic documents or separate authentication steps.

What is a CHSI degree verification report?

A CHSI degree verification report is an official online verification document used to confirm Chinese higher education degree information for overseas study, immigration, employment or credential evaluation.

What is a transcript verification report?

A transcript verification report confirms academic transcript information such as courses, grades, credits and institution details. It may be required for course-by-course credential evaluations.

Do CHSI reports have English versions?

Some China higher education verification reports can be issued or displayed in English, depending on the report type and platform options.

Can CHSI or CSSD reports support WES applications?

Yes. CHSI or CSSD degree and transcript verification reports are often relevant when preparing China academic records for WES or similar credential evaluation agencies.

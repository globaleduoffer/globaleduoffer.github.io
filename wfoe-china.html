What is a WFOE in China?

A WFOE (Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise) is a limited liability company in China that is fully owned by foreign investors. It is one of the most common structures for overseas founders who want operational control, the ability to hire staff locally, sign contracts, issue invoices (fapiao), and run day-to-day business activities in China.

WFOE vs. Representative Office vs. Joint Venture

Option Best for Key limitation
WFOE Operating business in China with full control Requires compliance (tax filings, annual reporting)
Representative Office Market research / liaison functions Cannot directly invoice/operate revenue-generating business
Joint Venture Partner-required industries / local collaboration Shared control and governance complexity

Exact requirements vary by city and industry. Most cases involve the following:

  • Company name options + proposed business scope
  • Registered address information (or address service if needed)

  • Notarization / apostille of overseas corporate documents (when applicable)

WFOE Incorporation Process

01. Setup & Naming

Selection of business scope, name pre-approval, and collection of shareholder documents (Apostille support included).

02. Formal Filing

Submission to the Market Supervision Bureau (SAMR) and Public Security Bureau for official company seal carving.

03. Post-Registration

Tax registration, social insurance account setup, and ongoing accounting services to maintain compliance.

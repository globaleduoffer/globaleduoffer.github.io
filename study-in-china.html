Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: How early should I start preparing for Study in China applications?
A: We recommend starting at least 6–12 months in advance (and earlier if you want scholarships). Early planning gives you time for language tests, school selection, document preparation, and deadlines.
Q2: Do I need Chinese language ability to study in China?
A: It depends on the program type. Chinese-taught degrees often require HSK. English-taught programs may focus on IELTS/TOEFL. We help you choose the right route based on your current language level and target schools.
Q3: How much are tuition and living costs in China?
A: Costs vary by city and university. Many bachelor’s and master’s programs range roughly from RMB 15,000–50,000/year. Living costs (housing, meals, insurance, daily expenses) also vary, and are often lower than many Western destinations.
Q4: What should I pay attention to for student visas (X1/X2)?
A: Ensure your admission notice and JW form details match your passport, prepare photos/medical/financial proof as required, and complete residence permit + local registration after arrival within the specified timeframe. We provide checklists and reminders.
Q5: Do you support students after they arrive in China?
A: Yes. Based on your needs, we can guide you through onboarding steps (registration, housing, insurance), and help coordinate with your school when communication support is needed.

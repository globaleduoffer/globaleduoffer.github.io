Use Cases

Who Needs WES Credential Evaluation Support?

WES credential evaluations may be used for immigration, study, employment, licensing and credential recognition in Canada, the United States, New Zealand and other destinations.

Canada Immigration

For Express Entry, PNP and ECA requirements where a Canadian education equivalency report is needed.

United States

For U.S. university admissions, employment screening, professional licensing or employer credential review.

New Zealand

For applicants preparing overseas education documents for New Zealand study, employment or migration-related use.

Chinese University Graduates

For applicants with China degree certificates, transcripts, graduation certificates and school-issued academic records.

6 Steps to Complete Your WES Credential Evaluation

1
Initial Inquiry & Background

Share your highest degree, institution, country of study and your purpose (Canada immigration, U.S. study or employment, New Zealand use, licensing or other credential recognition). We will assess which WES evaluation type and service scope best fits your needs.

2
Confirm Service Plan & Fee

Based on your situation we explain the required documents, estimated processing time and our service options. Once you confirm and pay the service fee via international transfer, we start working on your case immediately.

3
Prepare Documents & Translation

With our guidance you prepare transcripts, diplomas and other required documents. We handle certified translations and formatting, and perform an initial consistency check.

4
University Delivery & Online Submission

We guide you in requesting that your university or archive sends official transcripts to WES, and assist you in completing the WES online forms and document uploads correctly.

5
WES Review & Status Follow-up

After WES receives all required documents, your file enters the evaluation process. We help monitor status updates and provide suggestions if WES asks for additional information or clarification.

6
Report Issued & Next Steps

Once WES issues the evaluation report, we help you review the details and plan how to send or use it for your target universities, immigration authority, employer or licensing body, offering further advice when needed.

Delay Prevention

Common WES Delays We Help Applicants Avoid

Transcript Delivery Problems

Universities may send documents in the wrong format or through an unsupported channel, causing WES review delays.

Name or Date Mismatch

Small inconsistencies between passport, degree certificate, transcript and WES account details can trigger clarification requests.

Translation Format Issues

Incomplete or poorly formatted translations may lead to document rejection or repeated submissions.

Additional insight: avoiding delays in your WES evaluation

In practice, many applicants run into problems when universities are not familiar with WES procedures, when information is entered inconsistently, or when translations do not fully meet WES standards. These issues can lead to requests for additional documentation, repeated transcript submissions, and significant delays to immigration or study plans.

Drawing on our experience across a wide range of institutions and immigration programs, we help you choose the correct submission channels and ensure that each document is prepared in line with WES expectations. FAQ

WES Credential Evaluation Questions

Is WES only for Canada immigration?

No. WES is often used for Canada immigration, but WES evaluations may also be used for U.S. universities, employers, licensing bodies, New Zealand-related applications and other credential recognition purposes.

Can WES be used for the United States?

Yes. Many U.S. schools, employers and licensing bodies may request or accept a WES credential evaluation, depending on their own requirements.

Can you help with Chinese university transcripts?

Yes. We can help applicants review China degree documents, transcript requirements, translation needs and delivery options before WES submission.

Can you fix a WES document rejection?

We can review the rejection or clarification request, identify the likely issue and help prepare a corrected document strategy where possible.

6 Steps to Obtain Your China Police Certificate (PCC)

1
Initial Inquiry & Basic Information

Tell us which Chinese city you stayed in, your length of stay, visa type and destination country. We will assess feasibility, confirm the cities involved and provide an estimated processing time.

2
Confirm the Plan & Service Fee

After you confirm the quotation and processing route, you may pay the service fee via international transfer. We will immediately start preparing the necessary application documents for your China PCC.

3
Provide Supporting Documents

With our guidance you will prepare the required supporting documents, such as a power of attorney, scans of your current and previous passports, police registration forms, and other documents requested by local authorities.

4
Submission by Our Local Partner

Our local partners will submit your application in person at the relevant Public Security Bureau / Exit-Entry Administration, and closely follow up with the police office until the certificate is issued.

5
Certificate Issued & Details Checked

Once the Police Certificate is issued, we will send you a scanned copy (and English translation, if needed) for your confirmation, to ensure that all personal details and dates are correct before proceeding with notarization or legalization.

6
International Courier Delivery

The final original Police Certificate together with any notarial certificate, apostille or consular legalization will be sent to you by DHL / UPS or another trackable international courier to the address you provide.

Additional insight: why a China Police Certificate is so important

A China Police Certificate (PCC) is frequently requested for immigration, work permits, permanent residency, citizenship, overseas employment, and academic admissions. It is considered a core part of background checks by immigration authorities, visa centers, and employers. Because procedures and document formats vary across local Public Security Bureaus and notary offices in China, many applicants experience delays or rejections simply because the certificate does not fully meet the destination country’s technical requirements.

Designing the right approach based on your destination country

We review your personal situation, residence history in China, passport and visa records, and the specific requirements of the authority you are applying to. Based on this, we recommend the most appropriate route, including whether your PCC needs to be translated, notarized, apostilled, or fully legalized by a consulate. With our guidance, you can submit a China Police Certificate that aligns more closely with the expectations of immigration offices, embassies, and employers overseas.
